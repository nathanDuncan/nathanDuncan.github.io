    Projects

    RESEARCH

    Robora Lab

    UAV-USV Landing with Model Predictive Control

    Supervisor: Dr. Melissa Greeff

    February - June 2024

    This paper aims to solve a concensus rendez-vous problem for a heterogeneous robot team of an uncrewed surface vessel and unmanned aerial vehicle operating in harsh open waters. Co-authored by myself, Jess Stephenson, and Dr. Melissa Greeff, we develop a novel sequential distributed model predictive control scheme to coordinate a safe landing location and minimize tilt at touchdown.

    Defence Research and Development Canada

    Unmanned Multi-Agent Swarm Control

    Supervisor: Dr. Bumsoo Kim

    July 2024 - April 2025

    This project focuses on the robust control of Unmanned Aerial Vehicles opperating in close proximity to conduct cooperative missions in harsh environments. Agents must resist external disturbances while avoiding collisions within the swarm. The core of this project is an event-triggered hierarchal MPC controller.

    Robora Lab

    Ship-To-Shore: Mapping of drifting objects

    Supervisor: Dr. Melissa Greeff

    July - October 2023

    My first research project attempting to generate spatial-temporal maps of semi-static shoreline environments via unmanned aerial vehicles to track drifting objects and inform safe surface vessel docking routes. I designed mounting devices for the UAV sensor suite and performed large data set collections during field trials on Lake Ontario. The mapping and detection of drifting objects was performed using a LiDAR Inertial Odometry Smoothing and Mapping algorithm.

    DESIGN TEAMS

    Queen's Knights Robotics Team

    Turret Control Systems

    July 2023 - May 2024

    As project manager of the turret control sub team, I lead a small group of programmers to implement control of each robots aiming and firing system. Our team leveraged PID control algorithms to effectively transfer remote inputs to effective performance of the robot's loading, aiming, and firing actuators while also maintaining stability on top of the dynamic holonomic base.

    Queen's AutoDrive Team

    Systems Integration

    October 2022 - June 2023

    Acted as a general member of the Systems Integration sub team and liason to the Controls sub team. My role primarily employed the use of ROS2 to manage the input and output signals of the control team and allow programs to operate with improved time complexity. I also gained experience working with Geographic Information Systems, accessing datasets of superficial road elements to supplement navigation.

    Queen's Biomedical Innovation Team

    IV Insertion Training Module

    September 2023 - June 2024

    On this project, I acted as project manager of the electrical sub team with the objective to created a low cost training device for intravenous insertions. Our team designed systems to vary vein placement between trials, provide feedback queues and flashback to the nurses, and measure quantative information of the insertion including incident angle and quality of insertion.
